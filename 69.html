Fleece has a pile surface on both sides of the fabric, meaning each side has a layer of cut fibers. Air pockets can sit between the threads in this pile surface, meaning the material can hold in that bit more warmth. Not only is polyester fleece warm and durable, but it is moisture resistant making it ideal for extreme weather conditions or for sportswear which it became popular for in the 1990s, due to it being warmer than wool and much lighter to wear. What is Fleece Used For? This fabric has been used for everything from ear-warmers for baby calves, to underwear for astronauts. Its versatility makes it one of the most commonly used fabrics in the textile industry today. As mentioned, fleece is the ideal material for athletic garments. The cloth wicks perspiration and moisture away from the body, keeping athletes dry, while letting air circulate through the fibers. It has also been known to keep odors from sweating at bay!
