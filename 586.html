585 ● Overproduction The most serious of the wastes, overproduction can cause all other types of wastes and results in excess inventory. Stocking too much of a product that goes unused has obvious costs: storage, wasted materials, and excessive capital tied up in useless inventory. Depending, of course, on the product in question, overproduction can have very serious environmental effects. More raw materials than necessary are consumed; the product may spoil or become obsolete, which requires that it be tossed; and, if the product involves hazardous materials, more hazardous materials than necessary are wasted, resulting in extra emissions, extra costs of waste disposal, possible worker exposure, and potential environmental problems resulting from the waste itself. ● Inventory Inventory waste refers to the waste produced by unprocessed inventory.
