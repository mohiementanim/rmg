Preparation or Initialization Represented by an elongated hexagon, originally used for steps like setting a switch or initializing a routine. SPAGHETTI DIAGRAM A spaghetti diagram is defined as a visual representation using a continuous flow line tracing the path of an item or activity through a process. As a process analysis tool, the continuous flow line enables process teams to identify redundancies in the workflow and opportunities to expedite process flow. For example, areas where many walkways overlap are causes of congestion and delay. Waiting is one of the eight wastes of lean, because it is considered "unnecessary motion." The spaghetti diagram helps highlight major intersection points such as these that may not be noticed otherwise.
