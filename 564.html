563 ● Use existing knowledge, skills and resources in management, internal audit, compliance etc. ● Embed ERM into the fabric of the organization ● Take a holistic, portfolio view of risks across the enterprise ● Never treat ERM as a project – ERM is a process ● Don’t get bogged down in details and history – ERM should be strategic and forward-looking ● Avoid relying only on a few key staff – make ERM everyone’s job ● Don’t take a silo or stove-pipe approach to risks. Don’t ignore how risks might impact on other parts of the business ● Avoid obsessing too much about categorizing risks – rather than ensuring that the key risks have been identified and mitigation plans developed ● Never assume that the risk register is complete – there will always be ‘unknown unknowns’ and the biggest enemy of effective ERM is complacency
