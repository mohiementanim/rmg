482 understanding of the project. Steps to Construct an Interrelationship Digraph Let’s consider an example of a company that wants to source a new Electronic Resource Planning System (ERP). ERPs are databases used in large businesses to organize information and create reports across multiple departments. In this example, we will consider eight different departments found in a manufacturing environment. Step 1: Draft a Statement Draft a statement for the Interrelationship Diagram to explore. Consider using your Problem Statement found from the Project Charter if available. Write the statement on a white board for all participants to see. In this case, the statement is “Which Department is Most Impacted by an ERP System?” Step 2: Brainstorm Key Factors Next, have participants brainstorm key factors related to the statement. Each participant writes their ideas on a card or sticky note. You may gather ideas using the other 7M tools if created in advance. In the case of our ERP system, participants brainstormed eight departments that are primary users of the ERP system.
