683 OEE FACTORS Your OEE score tells you where you are at, but the three underlying factors (Availability, Performance, and Quality) tell you where you should focus your improvement efforts. Where do we start? Let’s start at the beginning – with All Time. This is also called 24/7 time (it includes every minute of every day). Chart of All Time if you were producing 24/7 From All Time, you subtract Schedule Loss, which includes all time that should be excluded from OEE analysis because there is no intention of running production during this time (e.g., plant shutdowns, breaks/lunches, or periods where there are no orders). Schedule Loss is part of TEEP (Total Effective Equipment Performance). It is not part of OEE. The remaining time is your Planned Production Time. OEE begins with Planned Production Time and scrutinizes all efficiency and productivity losses that occur within that time, with the goal of reducing or eliminating these losses. Chart of Planned Production Time, the time that manufacturing equipment is expected to produce Now we will look at the three OEE factors, each of which takes into account a different type of loss. They are Availability, Performance and Quality. OEE Availability Availability takes into account Availability Loss, which includes any events that stop planned production for an appreciable length of time (usually several minutes; long enough for an operator to log a reason).
