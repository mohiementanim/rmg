In 1999, Spear and Bowen identified four rules which characterize the "Toyota DNA": All work shall be highly specified as to content, sequence, timing, and outcome. Every customer-supplier connection must be direct, and there must be an unambiguous yes or no way to send requests and receive responses. The pathway for every product and service must be simple and direct. Any improvement must be made in accordance with the scientific method, under the guidance of a teacher, at the lowest possible level in the organization. This is a fundamentally different approach from most improvement methodologies, and requires more persistence than basic application of the tools, which may partially account for its lack of popularity.
